Dr. Tworit Dash

Postdoctoral Researcher at TU Delft, Netherlands

About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Microwave Sensing, Signals and Systems (MS3) group within the Department of Microelectronics at Delft University of Technology (TU Delft). My research focuses on advanced signal processing techniques for weather radar systems, particularly in the context of fast-scanning phased array radars.

Originally from Odisha, India, I earned my Bachelor of Technology in Instrumentation and Electronics Engineering from the College of Engineering and Technology, Bhubaneswar, in 2016. Following this, I worked at Robert Bosch India as an On-Board Diagnostics Calibration Engineer from 2016 to 2018.

In 2020, I completed my Master of Science (cum laude) in Electrical Engineering at TU Delft, specializing in Telecommunication and Sensing Systems. My MSc thesis, conducted in collaboration with ASTRON, focused on the computationally efficient design of conical horn antennas for the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) project.

I obtained my PhD from TU Delft in February 2025, with a dissertation titled "On Doppler Processing for Fast-Scanning Weather Radars." My current work involves developing electromagnetic inverse problem formulations and novel signal-processing algorithms for atmospheric parameter retrieval in the PHARA (Phased Array Radar for Atmospheric Research) project.

I have contributed to several publications in the field of weather radar signal processing and antenna design. Additionally, I have developed the TWORIT tool, which facilitates the calculation of S-parameters and far fields for complex waveguide structures.
