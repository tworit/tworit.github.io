About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Microwave Sensing, Signals and Systems (MS3) group at TU Delft, Netherlands. My research lies at the intersection of radar signal processing, atmospheric remote sensing, and applied electromagnetics. I earned my PhD at TU Delft in 2025 with a dissertation titled On Doppler Processing for Fast Scanning Weather Radars. Prior to this, I received an MSc in Electrical Engineering (cum laude) from TU Delft, where I worked on antenna design for the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) in collaboration with ASTRON.

I have authored multiple peer-reviewed journal articles and conference papers on radar processing, Doppler spectrum modeling, beamforming, and phased array weather radar systems. My industry experience includes working at Bosch as an On-Board Diagnostics Calibration Engineer. I am also the creator of the TWORIT simulation tool for modeling complex waveguide structures.
